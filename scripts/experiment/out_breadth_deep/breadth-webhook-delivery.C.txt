The proposed webhook delivery system has critical architectural flaws, primarily regarding infinite immediate retries and lack of URL validation. It is highly vulnerable to SSRF attacks, head-of-line blocking, and self-inflicted DDoS. A complete redesign is necessary to implement backoff, timeouts, and security boundaries.
1. **Infinite Retry & Head-of-Line Blocking**: Immediate, unbounded retries on failure will quickly exhaust the worker pool if a customer's endpoint goes down, halting delivery for all customers (noisy neighbor).
2. **SSRF (Server-Side Request Forgery)**: Allowing 'any URL' without validation permits attackers to target internal SaaS infrastructure (e.g., 127.0.0.1, AWS metadata endpoint) or perform port scanning.
3. **Lack of Exponential Backoff & Jitter**: Immediate retries will DDoS struggling customer servers, worsening outages rather than allowing recovery.
4. **No Worker Timeouts**: Tarpit servers that accept connections but don't respond can hang worker threads indefinitely, leading to total system lockup.
5. **Single Queue Architecture**: A single queue lacks fairness; a high-volume customer or failing endpoint can delay time-critical events for all others.
6. **No Dead-Letter Queue (DLQ)**: Without a maximum retry limit and DLQ, undeliverable messages will infinitely bloat the queue and consume resources forever.
7. **Missing Payload Signatures**: Customers cannot verify the authenticity of the payload without HMAC signatures, exposing them to spoofed events.
8. **DNS Rebinding Attacks**: The system is vulnerable to DNS rebinding if URL validation happens before resolution at the time of dispatch.
9. **Missing Circuit Breakers**: The system wastes resources continuously hitting dead endpoints instead of pausing delivery for that specific URL.
10. **Lack of Rate Limiting**: The system cannot throttle outgoing requests, potentially overwhelming both its own network egress and customer endpoints.
11. **No Payload Size Limits**: Extremely large payloads could cause Out-Of-Memory (OOM) errors on the workers.
