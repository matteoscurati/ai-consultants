The proposed webhook design has critical architectural flaws leading to immediate system collapse and severe security vulnerabilities. The lack of backoff, single queue architecture, and unrestricted URL registration guarantee cascading failures and SSRF exploits.
1. Server-Side Request Forgery (SSRF): Allowing 'any URL' permits attackers to target internal network addresses (e.g., 127.0.0.1, AWS metadata 169.254.169.254) or scan internal ports. 2. Infinite Retry Loop & Resource Exhaustion: Retrying 'immediately, repeatedly, until it succeeds' without a cap or exponential backoff will infinitely trap workers on permanently dead endpoints, effectively halting all processing. 3. Head-of-Line Blocking & Noisy Neighbors: A single shared queue means one customer's slow or failing endpoint will block deliveries for all other customers. 4. Unbounded Timeouts: Without strict HTTP connect/read timeouts, malicious 'tarpit' servers can hold worker connections open indefinitely. 5. Lack of Payload Authentication: Without cryptographic signatures (e.g., HMAC), customers cannot verify the authenticity of the payload. 6. Accidental DDoS: Immediate retries on a struggling customer server will overwhelm it further, ensuring it never recovers. 7. DNS Rebinding: Attackers can bypass naive URL validation by swapping IP resolution after initial checks.
